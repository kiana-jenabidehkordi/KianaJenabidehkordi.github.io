My Career Journey

The first time I dreamed of a career, I imagined myself in a white dress code, working in a lab or a hospital, so I decided to study pharmacy, a combination of lab and hospital. In January 2014, I started my Doctor of Pharmacy program, believing it would be my lifelong path. By 2018, I had completed my internship and started working in a hospital as a Pharm-D intern, excited to begin my career.

However, after three months, I realized pharmacy wasn't my true passion. I enjoyed the work, but it didn't light the spark I was looking for. While continuing in pharmacy, I started exploring other options, hoping to find something more adventurous.

In December 2018, I took my first step into entrepreneurship. A friend and I started a small cosmetic business, creating customized herbal creams, gels, and lotions for spas and salons. While we had some customers, our products didn't stand out enough to grow the business. So, in July 2020, we shifted to marine-based products, focusing on spirulina and phycocyanin.

This new venture came with its own challenges. Growing algae in a lab and scaling production turned out to be much harder than we expected. With limited money and resources, we couldn't keep the business running. By September 2020, we had to close it down.

Although it was disappointing, this experience taught me a lot. I learned the importance of understanding technology deeply, doing honest market research, and building a strong support network. Most importantly, I realized how much I needed to keep learning.

I worked as a lab assistant while improving my English and applying to master's programs in entrepreneurship and innovation. In 2021, I was accepted into Toronto Metropolitan University. At first, I attended classes online while working full-time. In December 2021, I moved to Canada, ready to start a new chapter.

The transition wasn't easy. The first six months were tough as I adjusted to a new culture and pushed myself out of my comfort zone. But I kept going. Over the next 18 months, I joined TMU's Innovation Boost Zone, started working on a new startup, and gained hands-on experience. After graduating, I needed to focus on practical matters like getting permanent residency and building the foundation of my life in Canada.

I paused my startup and took a job at TMU's Biomedical Zone, working with startups in the healthcare space. I discovered it is so joyful to work with researchers, inventors, and startups to help them in their startup development. I got to work with many cutting-edge technologies but not be overwhelmed with the stresses of managing a startup.

While working with Biomedical Zone, I was assigned to work with the Systems, Software, and Semantics (LS3) Lab as consultant and conduct market research for some of their projects. After two months of working with their team, they offered me the opportunity to become a team member on the Codify AI project. They hired me as an Innovation Specialist in Bridging Divides.

I was on a 6-month probation, during which I worked with Bridging Divides on two streams: first, as a product owner for Codify AI, focusing on user experience and aligning the product features with the stakeholdersâ€™ business strategy. Second, developing an innovation portfolio (innovative projects that contribute to innovation KPIs) for Bridging Divides. I proposed five projects, and three of them were funded.

After the probation period, I was hired as Innovation Lead. I managed a team of four students, executing and managing three projects that I had proposed: Build a Bridge, Startup Spotlight, and Heritage Market Place, while allocating half of my time to Codify AI. In 2024, when the BMO Newcomer Workforce Integration Lab was initiated, I was added to their team and began my collaboration with them.

I had an enjoyable experience working on the development of Codify AI. I learned that to continue my career in digital tools development, I need to learn more about the technical aspects of developing AI and ML products. I took a 16-week accelerated program at the University of Toronto, and in this program, I learned many new things every week. Every weekend, I would spend hours debugging code and applying all of my learning to practice and complete mini projects. I am very pleased with this intense learning experience.

I believe AI is fundamentally reshaping how industries operate, develop products, analyze customer data, and design strategic initiatives. I want to be a part of this transformation. Moving forward, I'm prepared to bring three key strengths to my future team: project management expertise, entrepreneurial thinking, and knowledge in data science and machine learning. I'm actively seeking project management positions with companies that are pioneers in AI adoption, where I can collaborate with teams to accelerate product development and contribute to AI integration in organizations.
